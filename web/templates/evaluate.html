4. Literature Alignment

References explicitly relevant to this evaluation report.

Report block Main references How they are used here
MSP baseline [8], [11] Used only as a crude pathology-relevant baseline for confidence/OOD comparison, not as a strong uncertainty method.
MC Dropout Gal and Ghahramani (2016) The implemented stochastic forward-pass method in this repository is based on dropout as a Bayesian approximation.
Deep Ensembles Lakshminarayanan et al. (2017), [8], [11] Deep ensembles are the core ensemble method source, while [8] and [11] justify pathology-specific shift and OOD evaluation.
Temperature scaling / calibration Guo et al. (2017) The post-hoc calibration method used in the report is temperature scaling in the standard classification setting.
Error detection, calibration, OOD metrics [8], [11] These are the strongest pathology-specific references supporting evaluation under realistic distribution shift and uncertainty-based discrimination.
Distribution shift / OOD in pathology [8], [11] Supports near-OOD/far-OOD analysis and pathology-specific shift robustness discussion.
  • MC Dropout: grounded in Gal and Ghahramani (2016).
  • Deep Ensembles: grounded in Lakshminarayanan et al. (2017), with [8] and [11] providing pathology-specific shift context.
  • Temperature Scaling: grounded in Guo et al. (2017).
  • Shift / OOD in pathology: grounded primarily in [8] and [11].
Reference Why it is kept on this page
[8] Linmans et al. (2023) Directly supports digital pathology OOD evaluation, patch-to-slide aggregation, and the near-OOD versus far-OOD framing used in this report.
[11] Thagaard et al. (2020) Directly supports uncertainty behavior under real pathology dataset shifts and the claim that deep ensembles are comparatively robust under shift.

The full ordered literature list remains on the Project page. This evaluation page keeps only the references that most directly justify the implemented methods and reported pathology evaluation setting.

5. Advanced Uncertainty Analysis

Three advanced analyses extending the standard evaluation: conformal prediction (coverage guarantees), aleatoric vs. epistemic uncertainty decomposition, and calibration degradation under distribution shift.

Conformal Prediction

Split conformal prediction uses the validation set to set a threshold guaranteeing that true labels fall in the prediction set with probability ≥ 1−α. Smaller average set size = more efficient (confident) predictions.

Aleatoric vs. Epistemic Uncertainty

Epistemic (model uncertainty, reducible) ≈ Mutual Information. Aleatoric (data noise, irreducible) = Predictive Entropy − MI. A high aleatoric ratio means the patches themselves are ambiguous, not that the model needs more training.
